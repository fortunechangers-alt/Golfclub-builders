Why Swing Weight Matters More Than You Think

Posted August 2025

Swing weight isn’t just a number—it’s a major factor in how your club feels, how you swing and ultimately how you perform. While total club weight tells you how heavy a club is, swing weight measures where that weight is distributed between the head and grip. A club with the right swing weight feels natural, boosts confidence and helps maintain a consistent tempo shot after shot.

What Is Swing Weight?

Swing weight is a relative measure of the balance point of a club expressed on a scale (D2, C9, etc.). It’s not a measurement of total mass, but rather how heavy the club feels when swung. Two clubs can weigh the same yet feel completely different if one has more weight concentrated in the head.

Why It Matters

• Consistency: A proper swing weight helps you repeat the same swing tempo, promoting consistent ball striking.
• Control: A balanced feel increases club‑head awareness, improving your ability to square the face at impact.
• Speed vs. Power: Lighter swing weights can increase club head speed; heavier swing weights offer more stability but may feel harder to swing. Finding your ideal balance gives you the best of both.

How Club‑Head Installs Affect Swing Weight

Changing to a heavier or lighter head shifts the balance point of the club. Even a few grams can alter feel dramatically. That’s why every head install in our shop includes a swing‑weight check and adjustment to dial in the feel.

Shaft Weight & Flex Influence

• Shaft weight: A heavier shaft moves the balance toward the grip, often lowering swing weight. Lighter shafts do the opposite.
• Flex: While primarily about shaft bend, flex can influence how the club loads and unloads at a given swing weight. Matching flex and swing weight ensures the shaft works harmoniously with your tempo.

Grip Weight Considerations

A heavier grip moves the balance point toward your hands, lowering swing weight. Lighter grips raise it. That’s why grip changes are always accounted for when we build or adjust clubs.

Tour Lock & Premium Weights

Premium counterweight systems, such as Tour Lock, allow fine tuning of swing weight without altering the club head or shaft. They insert weight into the butt end of the shaft. We offer both standard and premium swing‑weight adjustments, with only the cost of the weight itself added to labour.

Final Thoughts

The right swing weight makes your club an extension of your body. Next time you think about reshafting, installing a new head or swapping grips, remember that balance matters as much as total weight. We’re here to help you find the perfect feel for every club in your bag.
